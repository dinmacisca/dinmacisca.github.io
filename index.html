Project Timeline
APR 2022
Baseline Assessment
Audited existing reporting processes across all active sites. Identified that project data was being tracked in 6 separate unlinked spreadsheets with no standardised format.
JUN 2022
Dashboard Design & Build
Developed a centralised Excel master tracker with automated budget variance formulas and conditional formatting for red/amber/green alerts. Integrated with Power BI for executive reporting.
SEP 2022
First Inconsistency Report
Flagged a ₦4.2M procurement discrepancy at the Lekki Phase 1 site through budget variance analysis. Issue escalated to management and resolved within 3 weeks.
MAR 2023
Cross-Department Coordination Protocol
Introduced weekly data check-ins between construction, procurement, and finance departments to improve reporting accuracy and reduce reconciliation time.
DEC 2023
Final Performance Review
Delivered final annual operations report to management showing 18% reduction in budget variance and 31% improvement in on-time project milestones since dashboard implementation.
Key Findings & Recommendations
  • Lekki and Ikoyi sites consistently exceeded budget due to untracked material cost escalations — recommended monthly procurement reviews tied to inflation index
  • Construction department in Abuja reported 2–3 week delays in data submission, causing downstream reporting gaps — introduced standardised weekly reporting template
  • 24 data inconsistencies detected across sites; most common cause was duplicate supplier invoicing — flagged for finance audit
  • Pool installation projects (QuickPools brand) had shortest completion time of all project types — recommended as priority category for client acquisition
  • Water treatment projects had highest margin but longest timeline — recommended for client segmentation and premium pricing strategy
  • Abuja sites outperformed Lagos sites in budget adherence — attributed to smaller project scope and single-site focus

New Product Launch Support
Provided pre-launch market analysis for Ecobel's new premium synthetic engine oil SKU. Recommended target customer segments (commercial fleet operators and luxury car workshops) based on data insights.
OCT 2024
Post-Launch Performance Review
Tracked new SKU performance across 90 days post-launch. Reported 14% market penetration and identified top-performing distribution channels for scale-up recommendation.
Strategic Findings & Recommendations
  • Engine oils drove 78% of total revenue but showed signs of margin compression — recommended shift toward premium synthetic variants with higher margin profile
  • Auto detailing products significantly underperformed despite growing car care culture in Lagos — identified poor retail shelf placement as primary cause
  • TotalEnergies held dominant market share in mass segment; recommended Ecobel focus on premium niche where brand loyalty was lower and margin higher
  • Fleet operator segment (logistics companies, ride-hailing fleets) identified as high-volume, high-loyalty customer group largely untapped by Ecobel
  • Seasonal demand spike observed in October–December — recommended pre-season inventory build-up and promotional pricing strategy for Q4
  • New synthetic engine oil SKU achieved strongest performance in Lekki and Victoria Island — recommended geographic focus for Phase 2 marketing spend
